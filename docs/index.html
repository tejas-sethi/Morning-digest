ABC News Australia
New KPMG data shows Sydney's home ownership rate has fallen to its lowest point in seven decades, with affordability now beyond reach for most residents. The figures underline how dramatically Australia's largest city has shifted from a nation of homeowners to one of renters. The trend has major implications for household wealth, retirement planning, and the broader Australian economy.

The future of the Tasmanian site remains uncertain, raising questions about Australia's industrial self-sufficiency.

A world of higher inflation
The Spectator Australia
This piece argues that the era of structurally low inflation that defined the 2000s and 2010s was the historical anomaly, not the norm — and that businesses, investors and governments are making a serious mistake by treating it as the baseline to which we will inevitably return. The author examines the supply-side and geopolitical forces, from reshoring to resource nationalism, that are likely to keep price pressures elevated for the long term. It challenges the widely held assumption that central banks can simply engineer a return to 2% inflation without deeper structural reform.
