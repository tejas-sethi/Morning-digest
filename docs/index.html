Job system broken for workers, booming for private equity, report says
ABC News Business
A damning new report argues that Australia's employment services system is systematically failing the very jobseekers it was designed to help, while generating outsized profits for private equity firms and wealthy operators. The findings raise serious questions about the structural incentives built into a privatised welfare-to-work model — and who ultimately benefits when public contracts are handed to commercial providers. For anyone watching Australia's small business and investing landscape, the report is also a reminder of how government outsourcing can quietly redirect public money into private hands at scale.
Andy Burnham's missing economic strategy
UnHerd
Britain's newly installed Prime Minister Andy Burnham rode a wave of goodwill into Downing Street, but critics are already pointing to a conspicuous gap at the centre of his agenda: a coherent economic plan. The piece argues that Burnham's instinct for political mood music and community solidarity, while electorally effective, offers little guidance on the hard choices around growth, fiscal discipline and structural reform that a flatlining British economy demands. It challenges the widely held assumption that a politician's ability to connect emotionally with voters is sufficient preparation for governing through an economic crisis.
First-of-its-kind RBA paper outlines which businesses are most likely to be acquired
SmartCompany
The Reserve Bank of Australia has published a rare and unusually granular analysis of business acquisition patterns, drawing on data that has never before been examined at this scale to map out what acquirers actually look for when targeting Australian companies. For founders and small business owners thinking about an eventual exit, the findings offer unusually concrete signals about which financial and operational characteristics move the needle for buyers. It's the kind of research that typically circulates only in investment banking circles, now surfaced in a form that everyday entrepreneurs can use.
