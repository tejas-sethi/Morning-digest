ATO asked to justify 'extravagant' $73m Adelaide office move
ABC News Australia
The Australian Taxation Office is under fire for spending at least $73.4 million to relocate its Adelaide offices just four streets away into what's been called 'the best building in Adelaide.' Senators are demanding the ATO justify the expenditure at a time of fiscal scrutiny. It's a sharp reminder that government property decisions can carry serious public accountability consequences.
Sydney home ownership rate hits 70-year low
ABC News Australia
New KPMG data shows Sydney's home ownership rate has fallen to its lowest point in seven decades, with affordability now beyond reach for most residents. The figures underline how dramatically Australia's largest city has shifted from a nation of homeowners to one of renters. The trend has major implications for household wealth, retirement planning, and the broader Australian economy.
Concerns smelter closure exposes Australia to geopolitical risk
ABC News Australia
Australia's sole manganese alloy smelter has shut its doors, leaving the country more dependent on imported alloys critical to steel production. Industry and defence analysts warn the closure creates a strategic vulnerability at a time of heightened global supply chain tensions. The future of the Tasmanian site remains uncertain, raising questions about Australia's industrial self-sufficiency.
Argentina shatter England's World Cup dreams in semi-final
BBC Football
Argentina came from behind to beat England 2-1 in a dramatic World Cup semi-final in Atlanta, scoring two late goals to end Thomas Tuchel's side's tournament. Manchester United defender Lisandro Martinez featured for Argentina and has since attracted FIFA attention after players held a Falkland Islands banner post-match. The defeat has reignited fierce debate about England's tactical approach and whether anything has really changed since the Southgate era.
Backed into a corner by Iran over the Strait of Hormuz, Trump may resort to even more reckless escalation
The Conversation AU
US military strikes on Iran have entered their sixth consecutive night, yet analysts argue there is no viable military pathway to reopening the Strait of Hormuz — leaving Trump with escalating pressure and shrinking options. Iran's targeted campaign against commercial tankers is being described as highly deliberate rather than opportunistic provocation. This piece challenges the prevailing assumption that US military dominance can translate into a quick resolution of the Hormuz crisis, arguing that coercive force may only deepen the impasse.
