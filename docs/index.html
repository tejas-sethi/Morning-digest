Australia's highest paid CEO makes 500 times the average salary
ABC News Business
New data reveals Australia's top-earning executive is taking home nearly 500 times what the average full-time worker earns, reigniting debate about executive pay on the ASX. The figures come from the Australian Council of Superannuation Investors, which tracks remuneration across the country's largest listed companies. The gap has drawn fresh scrutiny from governance advocates and institutional shareholders.
Aussie investors fear millions in losses as alleged fake bond scheme unravels
ABC News Business
ASIC has taken investment firm Capital Guard to the Supreme Court, alleging it defrauded around 80 investors out of $17 million through the promotion of fake bond products. The regulator is seeking to have the company wound up and its assets frozen to protect what remains of victims' funds. The case is a stark reminder for retail investors to verify the legitimacy of fixed-income products before committing capital.
The US and Iran are back at war. And with one main sticking point, there's no end in sight
The Conversation AU
US-Iran hostilities have resumed in earnest, with Iranian forces launching missiles and drones at the US naval support base in Bahrain and both sides deadlocked over control of the Strait of Hormuz. Analysts warn the standoff has no clear off-ramp, with neither Washington nor Tehran willing to blink on the critical shipping chokepoint. For Australians, the conflict is already translating into higher fuel price forecasts as oil supply routes are disrupted.
Man Utd sign Tielemans for £35m from Aston Villa
BBC Football
Manchester United have confirmed the signing of Belgian midfielder Youri Tielemans from Aston Villa on a five-year deal worth £35 million. The 29-year-old brings Champions League experience and a reputation as a composed, technically gifted central midfielder — already being dubbed the "Belgian Frank Lampard" by some pundits. United are reportedly pursuing at least three further signings this window as the rebuild under their current regime continues at pace.
Batteries charge ahead as data centres fuel gas turbine costs: CSIRO
ABC News Business
CSIRO's latest GenCost report has found that battery storage has now undercut gas peakers as the cheapest way to meet peak electricity demand in Australia — a shift driven in part by surging gas turbine costs fuelled by the global AI-driven data centre boom. The findings mark a significant turning point for energy planning, with batteries previously considered unsuitable for meeting the sharp, unpredictable spikes that gas turbines were built to handle. This challenges the long-held assumption in energy policy that gas must remain the "firm" backup to renewables — suggesting batteries alone may now be sufficient for grid stability in many scenarios.
