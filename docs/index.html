ASX flat as oil price jumps, Telstra warns of post-outage scams
ABC News Business
The ASX closed the session essentially unchanged despite rising oil prices driven by renewed war-related tensions in the Middle East. Telstra also issued warnings to customers about a wave of scams exploiting confusion in the wake of a recent network outage. Investors will be watching closely as geopolitical risk keeps commodity markets on edge.
Businesses caught between Labor's trust tax and stamp duty
ABC News Business
Small businesses affected by Labor's new discretionary trust tax changes are in a holding pattern as state governments weigh whether to waive associated stamp duty fees. Industry groups are warning that relief will be limited unless all states sign on, leaving many owners uncertain about their next move. It's a pressure point worth watching for anyone running a family trust structure.
Ukraine and allies launch coalition to tackle Russia's ballistic missiles
Al Jazeera
Ukraine and nine allied nations have formed a new coalition specifically aimed at defending Europe against Russian ballistic missile attacks, drawing on hard-won Ukrainian expertise from over four years of full-scale war. The move comes as Russia has escalated its strikes in recent weeks, exploiting critical gaps in Ukraine's air defence inventory. It marks a significant step toward institutionalising Ukraine's battlefield knowledge within European security architecture.
Man Utd sign £48m Santos and close in on Tielemans
BBC Football
Manchester United have completed the signing of Brazilian midfielder Andrey Santos from Chelsea for around £48 million, adding a dynamic box-to-box option to their squad. The club is also in advanced talks with Aston Villa to bring in Youri Tielemans, with a medical reported to be imminent. United are shaping up for a busy summer rebuild with over £165 million potentially committed to midfield reinforcements.
US, Iran exchange attacks around Strait of Hormuz
Al Jazeera
The United States and Iran have exchanged military strikes in and around the Strait of Hormuz, with US CENTCOM releasing footage of drone boat attacks on an Iranian vessel in what it described as a first-of-its-kind operation. Iran's military has responded with warnings against any American interference in management of the strategically critical waterway. The escalation threatens one of the world's most important shipping chokepoints, through which roughly a fifth of global oil supply passes. This challenges the assumption that US-Iran tensions would remain at the level of proxy conflict and economic sanctions rather than direct kinetic exchange.
