Al Jazeera

ABC News Business

Were we lied to about skilled migration?
The Spectator Australia
This piece questions whether the economic case made to Australians for high levels of skilled migration has held up under scrutiny, examining whether wage growth, productivity, and housing affordability outcomes have matched the promises of successive governments. It argues that the framing of migration as an unambiguous economic boon may have obscured distributional costs borne unevenly by workers and renters. The article challenges the broadly held assumption that skilled migration programs are primarily calibrated to benefit the broader economy rather than specific industry and employer interests.
