Revolut Becomes Australia's Newest Bank After Securing Deposit Licence

Revolut has officially entered the Australian banking market after securing its deposit licence, moving quickly to launch business accounts offering variable interest rates up to 4.25%. The fintech giant is integrating its new banking features with its existing suite of tools, putting competitive pressure on incumbent banks and neobank rivals alike.

SmartCompany
Australian insurtech Cover Genius has crossed the double-unicorn threshold, securing a $143 million funding round that pushes its valuation past $2 billion. The former Smart50 winner has built an embedded insurance platform used by major global partners, cementing Australia's position as a serious player in global fintech.
Iran War Delays Upgrades to Queensland's Triple Zero Phone System
ABC News Australia
Queensland's critical Triple Zero emergency phone system upgrade has been pushed back to next year, with the ongoing Iran war cited as a contributing factor to supply and logistics disruptions. The delay raises serious concerns about the resilience of emergency infrastructure at a time when natural disasters are increasing in frequency across the state.
Man United Fan Groups Take Unprecedented Action Over Season Ticket Ban Threats
Manchester Evening News - Man United
Manchester United fan groups have issued a joint statement pushing back against the club's crackdown on ticket touting, warning that loyal, long-standing supporters are being caught up in heavy-handed enforcement and facing bans. The dispute reflects deepening tension between the Ineos-led ownership and the Old Trafford fanbase over how the club is being run off the pitch.
Why New Houthi Threats in the Red Sea Could Spell Trouble for Global Oil Prices and Inflation
The Conversation AU
Renewed Houthi activity in the Red Sea is threatening the Bab el-Mandeb Strait, a chokepoint through which roughly 10–12% of global shipping trade passes each year. Disruption to this route forces vessels on costly detours around Africa, pushing up freight costs and — in a still-fragile inflation environment — potentially flowing through to consumer prices worldwide. This challenges the assumption that Middle East conflicts are largely contained regional events with limited economic spillover for countries like Australia.
