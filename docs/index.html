Why the 'extreme male brain' theory of autism has been disowned
The Spectator Australia
Simon Baron-Cohen's once-influential 'extreme male brain' theory — which argued autism reflects an exaggerated drive for systemising over empathising — has fallen out of favour among researchers and clinicians. The piece examines the scientific and political reasons behind its decline, and what has replaced it in our understanding of autism's diversity. This challenges the long-held assumption that autism can be neatly explained through a single cognitive or neurological framework tied to gender.

The disturbing reality behind the affordable housing promise
ABC News Business
A Four Corners investigation has found that despite billions in government spending on 'affordable' housing across Australia, many of the resulting rentals remain out of reach for the single-income households who need them most. The investigation exposes a growing gap between the political promise of affordable housing and the lived reality for low-income renters. It raises serious questions about how 'affordability' is defined in policy — and who actually benefits.
Businesses caught between Labor's trust tax and stamp duty
ABC News Business
Small businesses restructuring away from discretionary trusts in response to Labor's new tax changes now face the added burden of stamp duty costs, with states yet to commit to meaningful relief. Industry groups warn that without state governments stepping up, many small business owners will be caught in an expensive bind not of their making. The situation highlights how federal tax reform can create unintended cascading costs at the state level.
Why you can't crush populism
UnHerd
This essay argues that mainstream political establishments keep misreading populist movements as temporary aberrations to be managed or outlasted, rather than as symptoms of genuine, unresolved grievances about representation and economic dignity. Each attempt to delegitimise or legally constrain populist parties tends to reinforce the very sense of elite contempt that fuels them in the first place. It challenges the comfortable assumption that better messaging — or simply waiting — is enough to restore trust in liberal democratic institutions.
