Why New Houthi Threats in the Red Sea Could Spell Trouble for Global Oil Prices and Inflation
The Conversation AU
Renewed Houthi activity in the Red Sea is threatening the Bab el-Mandeb Strait, a chokepoint through which roughly 10–12% of global shipping trade passes each year. Disruption to this route forces vessels on costly detours around Africa, pushing up freight costs and — in a still-fragile inflation environment — potentially flowing through to consumer prices worldwide. This challenges the assumption that Middle East conflicts are largely contained regional events with limited economic spillover for countries like Australia.

The prosperity paradox
The Spectator Australia
This piece examines the contradiction at the heart of Australia's economic story: by most conventional measures the country remains wealthy, yet living standards for ordinary Australians have been quietly eroding for years under the weight of inflation, stagnant wages, and policy drift. It argues that headline GDP figures and political talking points have papered over a slow deterioration that is only now becoming impossible to ignore. The article challenges the comfortable assumption that Australia's resource wealth and economic management have insulated everyday households from the structural problems plaguing other developed economies.

'Apple Upgrade' Program Reportedly Launching Next Week
MacRumors
Apple is partnering with Klarna to launch a new device financing scheme called 'Apple Upgrade' in the US on 28 July, covering iPhones, iPads, Macs and Apple Watches on 24- to 36-month terms. Customers will have options to pay off early, upgrade mid-term, or return the device at the end — though notably the new program will not bundle AppleCare+ with Theft and Loss coverage as the existing iPhone Upgrade Program does. Apple is simultaneously planning to wind down the old iPhone Upgrade Program, marking a significant shift in how the company structures its hardware financing.
